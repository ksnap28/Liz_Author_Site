Glossary



Glossary
Word Meaning
Agapia(e) beloved(s). The formal mistress of a wealthy man
Agora the central square in most cities. Usually includes the market, as well as government buildings and temples
Anazit A Lycendan boy’s mission to prove himself worthy of joining the meirat
Axoie (pl. axoiae) an unworthy person. A Lycendan slur.
Apasya’s Gift a drug that causes vivid dreams
Benicolum Myoan word. Term of endearment for a child, especially a son.
Cassato a dinner party for Gravan and Ythomin men. Often used as an excuse to hire hetaerae
Dimotorae a political party in Grava at the end of the Time of Tyrants. Has recently been reformed
Drachma(e) silver coin(s) used throughout Ennekos. Worth ten copper obols
Ekando the primary unit of Lycendan society. Literally translates to one hundred men
Ekantha a Lycendan officer. The leader of an ekando.
Erintha the Lycendan Minister of Peace (defense)
Famoia(e) a person (people) who speaks with the dead
Famonychte an Olenosi festival. Translates to Night of Souls.
Fiya(e) gold coin(s) used throughout Ennekos. Worth ten silver drachmae
Hetaera(e) courtesan(s)
Impluvium a sunken basin in the atrium of most Ennekian houses. This catches rainwater that falls through the compluvium and empties it into an underground cistern
Kyria a member of the Lycendan Women’s Council
Kyrios (pl. kyriotae) a member of the ruling council of Grava or Ythome
Kyriatha the president of the Lycendan Women’s Council
Kyriotha the president of the Ythomin Syndera council
Liver-eater greedy person
Machioraios a sword fighting competition held in many Ennekian countries
Maza barley dumplings.
Meanomis a Lycendan festival. Translates to Day of Names.
Meirat military training for boys in Lycenda
Mirza Myoan word. Honorific for a prince.
Obol a copper coin used throughout Ennekos. The lowest denomination.
Olantha The military dictator of Lycenda. Literally Minister of All.
Paides primary school for both girls and boys in Lycenda
Paiza a springtime festival in Ythome
Pater the legal guardian of any Ennekian woman. Usually a father or brother, but can be any man
Retsina a wine laced with pine resin
Sevilem Myoan word. Term of endearment, translates roughly to ‘beloved’
Sibyl a mythical priestess of Thao able to predict the future
Stratitha Lycendan Minister of Strategy (war)
Syndera the ruling council of Ythome
Thaveo/a Honorific for a priest/priestess of Thao
Thaveotha the head priest of Thao’s religion
Tsipouro a strong liquor distilled from grape pomace
Vohlo Myoan word. Vulgar insult
Voulis the ruling council of Grava
Vivliotas the great library in Larais
Wild Carrot Seed a contraceptive
